Feasibility Study for starting a Men’s Shed in Hamilton Ontario Canada

As part of a collaborative effort with the Hamilton Suicide Prevention Community Council (SPCCH), our team conducted a feasibility study to evaluate the establishment of a Men's Shed in Hamilton, Ontario. This initiative addresses the critical mental health challenges faced by middle-aged men, who are particularly vulnerable to social isolation and suicide. The project was undertaken by a diverse team including Nitheesha Reddy Terala, Ngoc Thuy Linh Vu, Philemon Anuroop Manda, and Adijat Ajeniya, with support from the McMaster Research Shop. The study aimed to create a sustainable community space where men could connect, engage in meaningful activities, and access mental health support in a non-stigmatizing environment.

Greenin Homes-Crafting Digital Experiences for Modern Living

We developed a comprehensive business case for "Greenin Homes-Crafting Digital Experiences for Modern Living" as part of my studies at Humber College's School of Applied Technology - Project Management Post Graduate Program. This project was completed as part of Group 4 in September 2024. The business case explores the digital transformation of a fictional Toronto-based interior design company, focusing on creating a professional website to expand their market presence beyond word-of-mouth referrals.

Construction of Hybrid Railway from Finch West Station to Humber College

The Finch West Station to Humber College Road Connection project will build a new road connecting Finch West Station to Humber College to improve local transportation and reduce traffic in Toronto, Ontario. This 12-month project will give locals, students, and everyday traveler's a long-term transit option. The project includes security protocols, environmental management, traffic control system deployment, and road building. The Ontario Ministry of Transportation, environmental organizations, and members of the local community are among the important stakeholders with whom it will be closely collaborating. It is anticipated that the new road connection will significantly improve accessibility, reduce travel times, and strengthen the local transportation system, which will ultimately help the local community as well as those who commute between Humber College and Finch West Station.

Smart Shopping Assistant – Loblaws

As part of a team project, we developed an AI-powered Smart Shopping Assistant for Loblaws, designed to enhance the online grocery shopping experience through intelligent recommendations, predictive insights, and personalized user journeys. Using Jira for sprint planning and task management, we organized the project into multiple sprints including MVP development, AI optimization, and promotional enhancements. We implemented agile methodologies to track progress with burn-up/down charts, velocity reports, and cumulative flow analysis. This hands-on project strengthened my skills in agile project execution, team collaboration, backlog grooming, and real-time sprint tracking using industry-standard tools like Jira.

Target’s Risk Response Plan for Canadian Re-entry

In this strategic risk response project, our team developed a comprehensive mitigation plan for Target’s potential re-entry into the Canadian retail market. We analyzed critical risks including tariff impacts, currency fluctuations, and supply chain disruptions—triggered by geopolitical and trade policy changes. The plan aligned with Target’s corporate strategy to regain market share and expand in North America. Key recommendations included supplier diversification, pricing adjustments, currency hedging, and enhanced supply chain resilience. This project deepened my understanding of qualitative and quantitative risk analysis, strategic alignment, and adaptive business planning in a global trade context.
